PROJ-001 · MATLAB · IMAGE PROCESSING
Sub-Pixel Bead Localization Tool
BU Bifano Lab / CELL-MET NSF
🔬

A MATLAB pipeline that automatically detects and localizes fluorescent microbeads in microscope images with sub-pixel accuracy — adapted from aerospace tracking methods.

  • Increased tissue-tracking capacity from 1 → 96 samples simultaneously
  • Achieved 1/50th-pixel accuracy (0.15 µm spatial precision)
  • 10-step pipeline: load → smooth → background subtract → Otsu threshold → Gaussian fit
  • Standard deviation of bead distances: 0.0819 px across 10 frames

PROJ-003 · OPTICS · NSF CELL-MET
Fluorescence Microscopy System for Cardiac Tissue
BU Photonics Center — Bifano Lab
💡
  • Captured fluorescent bead images using Grasshopper camera (0–255 AU, uint8)
  • Computed AU → ADU → electrons conversion using camera well depth, gain, dark noise
  • Validated Gaussian PSF model against theoretical Airy disk radial profile
  • Measured σx = 1.81 px, σy = 1.52 px; rotation φ = −0.033 rad
  • Theoretical accuracy: 1/50th of a pixel → 0.15 µm spatial precision
Fluorescence Microscopy PSF Characterization Optical Calibration Camera Calibration Laser Alignment
// Research Abstract — Presented ERN Conference 2026 (AAAS/NSF)

Cardiovascular disease remains the world's leading cause of death, and nearly a third of new drug candidates are withdrawn due to unforeseen cardiac toxicity. This work developed an automated sub-pixel optical localization tool for a 96-well plate imaging system — improving sensitivity of small-scale contraction dynamics and enabling smaller-scale constructs with 0.15 µm spatial precision.
